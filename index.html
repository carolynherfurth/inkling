INKLING

WILL YOU BOW OUT, FADE OUT, OR FLAME OUT?

10 questions that reveal where that inkling is pointing for the business you built.

An inkling starts as restlessness. A loss of interest. An annoyance. A desire to do something different. The sense that something that still works no longer works quite as well for you. You feel it before you can explain it. What comes next depends on whether you deflect, defer, or direct it.

Click your answer for each question below to find out which way your inkling is pointing.

DEFLECT

FLAME-OUT

The business is doing well. That's part of the problem — there's no obvious crack to point to, so instead of asking what needs to change, you ask something unanswerable: who even am I anymore. That question feels bigger and more important. It's also impossible to act on — which is exactly why it's easier to sit with than a concrete answer about the business. You keep going, because going has always worked before. Then one day, you're done.

ASK YOURSELF
  1. 1Do you consider yourself impulsive — especially once you've finally had enough?
  2. 2Do you yearn to do something different but can't picture what that next move would actually be?
  3. 3If you were earning the same (or more) income tomorrow without operating your business as-is, would you feel relieved — or disappointed?
DEFER

FADE-OUT

You know something has changed, but you don't know what to change. What once interested you no longer does. You tell yourself you should be happy with this — the business still works, and by every metric that matters, you should be. But no alternative feels compelling enough to act on. So you keep going — not because you've decided to stay, but because you haven't found a next move worth choosing.

ASK YOURSELF
  1. 4Have you been experiencing a prolonged sense of boredom in your business?
  2. 5Do you stay out of obligation — to clients, your team, or the identity you've built — more than actual desire?
  3. 6Is there a decision about your business you've been avoiding because you don't like the only options you can see from where you sit?
DIRECT

BOW-OUT

You get inklings like every other founder. The difference is you look at yours while you still have choices — before it's forced on you. That doesn't mean assuming the whole business has to end, or having to already know the answer. It means looking closely enough to see what's changed, what's run its course, and what happens next. You're the one driving it. Nothing gets decided by default.

ASK YOURSELF
  1. 7Do you have a clear-eyed objectivity about when it's time to exit your business?
  2. 8Are you driving your next decision as opposed to waiting for the business to force it?
  3. 9If someone noticed your blind spot, would you actually hear it — or would you get defensive?
